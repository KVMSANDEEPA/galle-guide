Discover the most engaging places

Let's see the Galle with us with you and your family.

Jungle Beach

A secluded beach near Unawatuna, perfect for a peaceful day by the sea. You can swim, snorkel, or simply enjoy the serene environment.

GALLE FORT

UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Galle Fort is the most iconic landmark in the city, built by the Portuguese in 1588 and fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century. Explore the Ramparts: Walk along the ancient walls with stunning views of the ocean. Lighthouse: The Galle Lighthouse, located within the Fort, is one of the oldest in Sri Lanka. Museums & Galleries: Within the fort, you can visit the National Maritime Museum and explore art galleries and boutique shops.

Koggala Lake


A short drive from Galle, this lake is dotted with small islands and is a great spot for boat trips and birdwatching. You can also visit the famous Martin Wickramasinghe Folk Museum here.

Dutch Reformed Church


One of the oldest Protestant churches in Sri Lanka, dating back to 1755. The church has fascinating architectural features and historical significance.

Galle International Stadium


If you’re a cricket fan, you’ll love visiting this picturesque stadium, known as one of the most beautiful cricket grounds in the world.

Unawatuna Beach


Just a short distance from Galle, this beach is famous for its golden sands and calm waters, making it ideal for swimming and relaxing.

Japanese Peace Pagoda


Located on a hill near Unawatuna Beach, this stunning white pagoda offers panoramic views of Galle, the ocean, and surrounding greenery.

The Sea Turtle Hatchery


Located about 5 km from Galle, this conservation project allows visitors to learn about sea turtles and their preservation efforts.

Old Dutch Hospital


A beautifully restored colonial building now home to cafes, restaurants, and boutique shops. It’s a perfect spot for dining or shopping with a view of the ocean.

Historical Mansion Museum


This private museum inside Galle Fort showcases a collection of antique items that tell stories of Galle’s colonial past. These sites offer a mix of history, nature, and leisure, making Galle a fascinating destination to explore.

Ariyapala Masks Museum – Ambalangoda


About 30 km from Galle, this museum in Ambalangoda is dedicated to traditional Sri Lankan mask-making, showcasing the country’s rich cultural heritage. You can learn about the ancient art of mask carving and its role in rituals and folk theater.

Hikkaduwa Beach & Coral Sanctuary


About 20 km from Galle, Hikkaduwa is famous for its coral reefs, making it a great spot for snorkeling and diving. The beach also offers plenty of water sports, lively restaurants, and vibrant nightlife.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve


A bit further inland, Sinharaja is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the last remaining rainforests in Sri Lanka. A day trip here will immerse you in rich biodiversity, perfect for nature lovers and bird watchers.

Mihiripenna Beach


A quieter alternative to the more popular Unawatuna Beach, Mihiripenna offers a more tranquil atmosphere with clear waters, ideal for swimming and relaxing.

Meeran Mosque


One of the most notable mosques in Galle, located within the fort, Meeran Mosque blends elements of Islamic architecture with colonial styles. It’s worth visiting for its serene beauty and historical significance.

Thalpe Beach


About 10 km from Galle, Thalpe Beach is known for its natural rock pools. It’s a peaceful stretch of coastline, ideal for a quiet day away from the more crowded beaches.

Kanneliya Forest Reserve


A bit further inland, Kanneliya is part of Sri Lanka’s rainforest ecosystem and is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. A visit here offers serene hiking trails, waterfalls, and opportunities to spot endemic species of birds and wildlife.

Madol Duwa Island


Famous from Sri Lankan literature, Madol Duwa is an island in Koggala Lake. You can take a boat ride to the island, which is associated with a famous novel by Martin Wickramasinghe, adding a literary touch to your visit.

Martin Wickramasinghe Museum of Folk Culture


Located in Koggala, this museum showcases traditional Sri Lankan village life. It offers an interesting collection of artifacts related to literature, folk music, and ancient tools.

Rumassala Hill


Near Unawatuna, this hill is steeped in legend and offers beautiful views of the surrounding coastline. According to local folklore, it is part of a mountain that was dropped by Hanuman during the Ramayana.
